<p><p>Malignant ascites treatment encompasses various approaches to manage fluid accumulation in the abdominal cavity due to cancer. Paracentesis involves the needle removal of excess fluid for symptomatic relief. Chemotherapy targets underlying cancer cells causing ascites, either systemically or locally. Surgical treatment may include techniques like peritoneovenous shunting to facilitate fluid drainage. Other treatments can involve the use of diuretics or supportive care measures. Each method aims to improve quality of life and mitigate symptoms associated with malignant ascites.</p></p>
